Trumbull Congregational Church, established in 1730, the oldest parish in Trumbull, CT. The church shown here is the 3rd church that was built in 1842, which was later destroyed by fire in 1898 and replaced but at a different location. The original church was on Church Hill Road next to the old Town Hall, which today is known as the Helen Plumb Building.

Trumbull was settled as a part of Stratford, CT, after 1639. The northern parts of present-day Trumbull were purchased from the Paugussett Indian Tribe in 1661 and 1662 by Stratford selectmen.

Will

In the name of God amen. I Ichabod French of the town of Brutus in the County of Cayuga and State of New York being of sound mind & memory & understanding do make and publish this my last will & testament in manner following that is to say, First. I will and direct that all my debts be paid from my estate. Second. I give and bequeath unto my son Hiram and Susan his wife the use and occupancy of the farm they now live upon situate in the town of Cato during their natural lives, and after their deaths. I give and bequeath the same farm to there heirs. Third. I give and bequeath to my son Charles Two hundred dollars payable in five equal annual payments from and after my death without interest. Fourth, I give and bequeath to my daughters Harriet Bennett and Minerva Cable, each one hundred dollars, and to My daughter Polly Treat fifty dollars, and to my daughter Fanny Bennett, twenty five dollars, all the above payable in five Equal annual payments from and after my death without interest Fifth. I give and bequeath to my wife Abagail the use and occupancy of one third part of my home farm and the buildings thereon and as much of the personal property as she would be entitled to by law during her natural life, Or one hundred and fifty dollars annually during her life as she may choose. Sixth. I give & bequeath to my son James all my estate both real & personal which have not been bequeathed in this will, and direct that he pay off the legacies as herein bequeathed from my estate, Seventh. In order to prevent difficullity and undo understanding in regard to this my last will and testament, I will ordain and direct that no account or changes shall be brought in by or against either of the desires before named. Eighth. And Last I hereby appoint my son James Executor of this my last will and testament. In witness whereof I have here unto set my hand and seal this twelfth day of February

1845 Ichabod French (SS)
