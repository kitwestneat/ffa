Genealogy of James French Sr.

1.1 James French Sr., b. 1710 of Lancaster Co., PA, d. Sep 1764 in Hanover, Lancaster, PA [20], m. Margaret in 1729 in Lancaster, PA, at age 19. They had 11 children:

2.1 Mary French, b. ca. 1730 in Lancaster Co., PA.

2.2 May French, b. ca. 1730 in Lancaster Co., PA.

2.3 Thomas French, b. ca. 1732 in Lancaster Co., PA. On 6 Sep 1769, Thomas French of Hanover Twp., Lancaster Co., PA, deeds to George Kinkead of Middleton Twp., Cumberland Co., PA, 197 acres surveyed and granted 10 May 1767, on Sherman’s Creek, adjacent to Robert Pollock, widow.

2.4 Isabel French, b. ca. 1734 in Lancaster Co., PA, m. James Low on 25 Jan 1754.

2.5 Agnes French, b. ca. 1736 in Lancaster Co., PA.

2.6 Elizabeth French, b. ca. 1738 in Lancaster Co., PA, m. Joseph White ca. 1758 in Lancaster, PA. Joseph White was b. 1740 in Strasburg, Lancaster Co., PA [20].

2.7 John French, b. ca. 1740 in Lancaster Co., PA, m. Jane Rutter on 3 Jan 1771 in Lancaster Co., PA, at St. James Church [19]. “An” Ann French m. Jacob Manley on 5 Aug 1778 in Lancaster Co., PA.

2.8 Sarah French, b. ca. 1742 in Lancaster Co., PA.

2.9 James French Jr., b. ca. 1744 in Berks Co., PA, d. before 25 Feb 1815 in Allegany, MD, m1. ca. 1762 to Jane Cowan (1742-25 Feb 1815 in Allegany Co., MD) of Berks Co., PA. James m2. Jane Humbertson in Apr 1811, only 4 years before he died, according to Allegany Co, MD marriage records 1791-1847. James French and Jane Cowan had children Daniel, Lot, Ralph, Benjamin, Elizabeth, James, and Mary.

James died intestate with 8 young children and his farm estate in PA. The court stepped in - all the children are named. Then his son Daniel died intestate and Daniel’s son Stephen stepped in to try to straighten it all out about 1833. The estate of James French was in the Orphan’s Court from Jan 1834 thru Aug 1834. Later his will was found in Allegany Co., MD where he must have died. The will was dated 13 Feb 1815. By 1834, most of his children had died: Daniel, Lot, Benjamin, Elizabeth, Mary.

James made his will in Allegany Co. on 13 Feb 1815, and bequeath to Jane French, his beloved wife $150, and to all his children his real property lying and being in the Commonwealth of the state of Pennsylvania and the County of Bedford in Providence township to be equally divided amongst them all.

3.1 Daniel French, b. before 1762 in Berks, PA. He was James’ first son. Daniel married a woman who was part Indian, named “Little Flower” [18], and of the Seneca Nation Indian Tribe, and she was not Indian but a mulatto or aborigine, but no proof [21]. The Indian is said to be a cover for the mulatto according to some members of the family [21]. However, the Seneca Nation is from NY, not PA. Died before 1834.

1790 -- Daniel French is also listed in the 1790 census of Bedford, PA. A bit more information is given in this census: 1 free white males of 16 years and upwards including head of families, 1 free white males under 16 years of age, 5 free white females including head of families, no other free persons, no slaves. The one free white male under 16 years of age in 1790 may have been Daniel French is also listed in the 1800 census in Providence and Colerain, Bedford, PA, 1 0 1 0 1 | 4 2 1 1 0. James French is also listed as a head of household in the same vicinity, as well as John French on another page. Males and Female columns: Under 10, 10-15, 16-25, 26-44, 45+.”

1810 -- Daniel is also listed in the 1810 census of Providence, Bedford Co., PA, with no other French member living in that township. 0 1 1 0 1 | 1 1 5 0 2

1820 -- There is a Daniel French listed in the 1820 census of Providence, Bedford Co., PA. Listed as neighbors of his are Nathan French and next to him is Ellener French, who appears to be quite elderly.

1830 – The 1830 census shows Stephen French living in Providence – he would have been 30.

1840 – The 1840 census shows Stephen French living in Providence – he would have been 40.

1850 -- Daniel French is also listed in the 1850 census of West Providence, Bedford Co., PA, b. ca. 1843 in PA. This census shows a Stephen French, farmer, age 50 and his wife Sarah French, 46. Their children are Rachel (20), James (18), Samuel (16), Mary (14), Phoebe (13), Daniel (7), Elijah (5), and Rachel French (57).

1880 – This census lists many other French members in Providence, all born in PA.

The following data is from Ref. [13] which states that Daniel died leaving 9 orphan children. The orphan court states that Nathan was his oldest child, and it listed Nathan’s grandchildren. It also states that Naomi died leaving 2 children. Nathan was deceased before his father’s death. James left a tract of land situated in Providence township, containing about 140 acres, adjoining lands of Jacob Fore, John Manspeaker, Raisin Manspeaker, and others, having one small old house and a small double barn thereon and about forty acres, cleared land. The next 7 children were daughters; therefore, son Stephen took over to straighten out the will. In 1834 all the children, grand children, and great grand children had not agreed upon a division of said land among themselves, but continued to hold the same together and undivided.

1785 Tax Assessment, Providence Township, Bedford, PA

James French, 50 acres [2]

Daniel French, 30 acres [2]

Jeremiah French, 50 acres [2]

Lott French; Single Freemen [2]

Daniel and James remained in Bedford. Daniel filed for a Revolutionary War Pension and was living in Bedford, PA, at that time [2]. Daniel and James are both listed in the 1800 Providence, Bedford County, PA census. They are also both listed in the 1790 census. Only Daniel is listed in the 1810 census. Daniel may be a brother to Jeremiah because both served in the Rev. War from Hunterdon, NJ [2]. This could be totally incorrect, for until proven, we are keeping Jeremiah in FFA Chart #191.

Lott French’s sister was Naomi French. She d. ca. 1829. Lot French was living in Hamilton Co., OH between 1806-1809.
