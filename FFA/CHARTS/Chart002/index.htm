To this genealogical tragedy of errors several professional and amateur genealogists have contributed by stating that Lieut. William French of Cambridge was baptized in Halstead, Essex Co., England, March 15, 1603 and was descended, through three generations of the name of Thomas, from Thomas Frenche, the elder, of Wethersfield, County Essex England, who died in 1599. Yet Mrs. Elizabeth (French) Bartlett, descendant of Lieut. William French, and "Record Searcher in England for the Committee on English Research of the New England Historic Genealogical Society, 1908-1917," published in New England Historical & Genealogical Register, Vol 65 pp. 284-286, proof that the William French who was baptized March 15, 1603, in Halstead, Essex Co., England, died in London in 1621, unmarried, and that his estate was administered by his brother, John, and then his brother, Edward, when John died, and then sister Elianor when Edward died.

History and Research

The main portion of this chart is taken from Beyers book [1]. After only four generations in America, this family spread from MA to CT, VT, NH, NY and PA. At present the descendants of William French may be in most U.S. states, including Mara French in CA. At present we have over 50 members who are descended from William French.

This chart is in no way complete; however, you can view various websites showing this line in detail. Just remember that this William was born in 1606, not in 1603. The William French who was born in 1603 died in 1621 and is detailed on FFA Chart #EB.

Intermarriages

Since the French family emigrated soon after the Pilgrims landed and there were not many families nor marriage choices in America, intermarriages in the French family were common. Here is an intermarriage between Chart #1 and Chart #2. Note that this does not change the male French DNA test results.

 

(1) Thomas French, Ch1., m. Susan Riddlesdale

(1) William French, Ch2, m. Elizabeth

(2) Sarah French b. 1638 in Cambridge, m. Jonathan Peake, b. 1637. They married 15 Aug 1660 in Roxbury, MA

(3) Jonathan Peake

 

First Generation

1.1 Lieutenant William French, christened 6 Apr 1606 in Halstead, England, as the son of William French (see his ancestry in England). See image below, D/P 96/1/1, Image 38, from the ERO (SEAX).

William m1. Elizabeth French (nee French) date unk. A proven marriage record has not yet been found. Elizabeth French (nee French) was chr. 30 Oct 1603 in Terling, England (see her ancestry in England), d. 31 Mar 1668. This entire paragraph needs far more research – it may be totally false.

William m2. 6 May 1669 Mary, dau. of Thomas and Sarah Lothrop (name later written Lathrop [4]) and widow of John Stearns. William d. 20 Nov 1681.

Of his 13 children, only six were living at the time of his death: John, Jacob, Samuel, Mary, Sarah, Hannah. Will made 5 Jun 1679, proved 20 Dec 1681. Williams wife, Mary, was bapt. 4 Oct 1640. She m1. Dec 1656 John Stearns of Billerica as his second wife. Mary had five children by this first marriage. It is not known who they lived with after John Stearns d. 5 Mar 1668/9. Children were Isaac Stearns b. 17 Apr 1658 d. young; Samuel Stearns, b. 3 Sep 1659; Isaac Stearns b 23 Dec 1661; Nathaniel Stearns b. 30 Nov 1663; Thomas Stearns b. 6 Dec 1665.

Second Generation

Children of William and Elizabeth French (nee French), 1.1

2.1 Francis French, (maybe nephew), b. in England ca. 1624, immigrated at age 10 [4] with parents in ship "Defence"; removed to Milford, CT about 1650, and four years later was one of the settlers in Derby, CT, m. 10 Apr 1661 Lydia Bonnell of Milford. They had nine children. Francis d. 14 Feb 1681 (a few months before his father) or 1691 [4]. Lydia d. 1 Apr 1708. Francis nor his children are mentioned in his fathers will of 1681 even though his father states that he is giving to all his grandchildren [1] p. 53. This line continues as FFA Chart #8.

2.2 Elizabeth French, b. in England ca. 1629, baptized 5 Sep 1630 in Halstead, England at the parish church of St. Andrews [6], but this record could not be found. She immigrated at age 6 [4] with parents, m1. Robert Eliot/Elliot of Dedham [1] prior to Nov 1658 [4] on 19 Sep 1650 [6], m2. Richard Ellis [1] p. 53. Richard Ellis is listed in his father-in-laws will. Elizabeth died prior to her fathers death of 1681.

2.3 Mary French, b. in England ca. Jan 1633, immigrated age 2 1/2 [4] with parents, baptized between two and three years of age at her fathers "joyning", m. Jonathan Hyde, d. 27 May 1672 at the birth of her son Joseph, who was her 12th or 14th child. First child was Jonathan Jr. who was mentioned in William (1) will along with his father, Jonathan Hyde Sr., therefore, both were living in 1681.

2.4* John French, b. in England ca. Feb. 1635, immigrated age 5 mo. [4] with parents, m1. 21 Jun 1659 to Abigail Coggan, dau. of Henry of Barnstable. Abigail d. 5 Apr 1662. John m4. 16 Jan 1677/78 Mary, dau. of Francis Littlefield of Woburn, MA and widow of John Kittredge of Billerica. Mary d. 1719. John was a colonel in the militia, and was often in the Towns service. He was wounded by the Indians in an assault on Quaboag in 1675, d. Oct 1712.

2.6* Jacob French, b. in Cambridge, MA, 16 Mar 1640, m1. 20 Sep 1665 Mary Champney and had ten children. Mary d. 11 Apr 1681, one month after her 10th child was born. Jacob m2. 2.7 Hannah French, b. in Cambridge, MA [4] 12 Apr 1641, d. 20 Jun 1642.

2.8 Hannah (second) French, b. 16 Feb 1644, m. in Braintree, MA [4] John Brackett 6 Sep 1661, had nine children, d. 9 May 1674. Daughter and John Brackett mentioned in William (1) will.

2.9 Samuel French, b. in Cambridge, MA [4] 3 Dec 1645, d. 15 Jul 1646.

The Samuel French who was b. 1653, m. Sarah, dau. of John Cummings, Sr. 28 Dec 1682 [5] in Chelmsford, MA, d. 4 Nov 1727 is not part of this family. Through research in Sep 1992, records show that this Samuel was the son of Richard French of Cambridge, born in 1653. Due to Richards felony and possible prison term in 1654 and consequent move out of the area, Samuel was given guardianship by his uncle, William.

Memorial to William French, 1775

"In memory of William French, son of Mr. Nathaniel French,

   Who was shot at Westminster March y'e 13th 1775 by the hands

   of Cruel Ministerial tools of George y'e 3d, in the Court

   House, at 11 o'clock at night, in the 22d year of his age.

 

  "Here William French his Body lies

   For murder his blood for vengeance cries

   King George the third, his tory crew

   Tha with a bawl his head shot threw

   For liberty and his country's good

   He lost his life and dearest blood."]

Symmes Family

Research done in 1991 which needs many updates and perhaps corrections.

FIRST GENERATION

1.1 Randolph (Randall) Symmes, b. ca. 1580, most likely m. a lady from the Davenaunte family of London or a lady from the Man family of London. Randolph had brother Richard Symmes of Welford. Randolph and Richard were cousins of Thomas Hewett, a citizen and clothmaker of London, who made his will 10 Oct 1575, proved 19 Jun 1576, buried in the parish church of St. Clement where he was a parishioner. Randall made his will on 8 Nov 1599 at the Parish of St. Lawrence Pountney in London [2]. He mentions Elizabeth in this will. Perhaps this Randall was actually another generation back. Randall was possibly of Hertfordshire.

SECOND GENERATION

2.1 Elizabeth Symmes, b. ca. 1603 in England, m. William French, immigrated with him and their four children on the Defence from London in 1635 to Cambridge, MA. Listed as age 32 on the immigration records. Ref [2] shows no marriage between Elizabeth and William at St. Clement in London between 1539-1640. It also shows that none of their children were born at St. Clement between these dates. In fact, no French is listed at all. No surname Symmes (or any similar spelling) is listed in Earles Colne, Essex County between these dates.

2.2 Sara Simmes, b. ca. 1605 in England, immigrated on the Defence from London in 1635 to Cambridge, MA. Listed as age 30 on the immigration records. Saras cousin, Anne Scroggs of Earles Colne, Essex, made her will out on 28 August 1641 giving to "my cousin Sara Simes (who is now in New England) five pounds" [1]. Anne Scroggs also gave to Richard and William Harlakenden, possibly brothers of Roger Harlakenden. Roger immigrated on the Defence from London in 1635 to Cambridge, MA. William Harlakenden was executor of Anne Scroggs will, and referred to him as her brother in law of Earles Colne. Sara had a grant of land in Cambridge in 1639 and died there 10 Jun 1653 according to Savage. Sara made her will 4 April 1653 and mentions "brother William French." Saras relationship to the Scroggs family was through the Burton family. Anne Scroggs sister, Smith Scroggs, m. William Harlakenden, son of Thomas and Dorothy (Cheney) Harlakenden. William d. 18 Mar 1674. Smith Harlakenden was buried 28 Jun 1651. Thomas Stanley m. Mary, widow of John Scrogges, mother of Edward Scrogges, and grandmother of Anne Scroggs.
