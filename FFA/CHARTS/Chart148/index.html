History and Background

Towns of Hartford County, Connecticut

First Generation

1.1* Elam French, b. 16 Jun 1791 in East Windsor, Hartford Co., CT, d. 10 Oct 1850 in Brandon, Rutland Co., VT, buried at the Pine Hill Cemetery, m. Rebecca Allyn ca. 1814 in the Congregational Church, Windsor, Hartford Co., CT; Rebecca Allyn was b. 18 Dec 1793 in Windsor, Hartford Co., CT and d. 11 Sep 1882 in Addison Co., VT. Rebecca was one of 9 children born to George Allyn and Sabra Griswold Case Allyn.

Rebecca AdleyÕs sister Louisa French (2), b. 24 Dec 1827 in Somerset, Windham Co., VT, d. 9 Aug 1912 in Chittenango, Madison Co., NY, m. Clement Samuel Cook. ElamÕs parents moved from CT to MA sometime between 1792 and 1798 when ElamÕs brother was born, then they returned to Hartford, CT.

In the 1850 census (just before Elam died), Elam is listed as age 59, b. ca. 1791 in CT, living in 1850 in Brandon, Rutland Co., VT with his wife Rebecca (57), and their children Adley (20), Mary and Martha (twins born in 1836 in Brandon) (14), and Ellen (9).

Elam French was a farmer born in CT; all his children were born in VT as per the 1850 census. On 28 Aug 1827 Elam bought a 1770 farm in Wilmington, VT, ÒThe Nutmeg InnÓ which he purchased on 28 Aug 1827. It is now a Bed and Breakfast at 153 Route 9 West. See http://www.nutmeginn.com/.

 

 

On October 3, 1958, Mr. Dave French purchased the property and turned it into a ski lodge to house skiers for the newly opened resort called Mount Snow. Dave named the property The Nutmeg Inn after his home State of Connecticut. In 1984 the Lawrence Family bought the Inn.

Elam d. 10 Oct 1850 in Brandon, Rutland Co., VT and was buried at the Pine Hill Cemetery in that city. His wife, Rebecca, was b. 18 Dec 1793 in Windsor, Hartford Co., CT, and d. 11 Sep 1882 in Brandon, Rutland Co., VT. .

1.2 John French, b. ca. 1798 in MA, living in 1850 in Hartford, Hartford Co., CT with his wife Jane French (50), and their son William French (17, born in CT). John was a teamster. He is listed in the 1850 census, age 52. John m. Abigail Adams.

1.3 Daniel French, b. 18 Dec 1785, d. 29 Mar 1846 in MA, life-long resident of E. Windsor, Hartford Co., CT. He was buried at the Town Street Cemetery in East Windsor.

The 1790 census of Hartford Co., CT shows a Daniel French living in Berlin town. The 1820 census shows a Daw French and a John French both living in East Windsor, Hartford, CT. The 1830 census shows Daniel French living in East Windsor, Hartford Co., CT; likewise the 1840 census. Daniel m1. Chloe Drake and had a daughter, son Daniel, son Elam who resided in Haydenville, CT, son Allen, and John Fitts who was adopted by Chloe. He m2. Nancy Drake. She was his 2nd wife, his 1st was Cynthia Frost who left him an infant daughter, Laura. Issue by Nancy are Nancy. Nancy and Chloe Gideon were sisters.

Daniel French m. Cynthia Frost on 29 Nov 1811. Daniel d. 4 Jul 1811, age 25. Daniel m. Nancy Drake on 29 May 1817, she d. 12 Jan 1823, age 31 in childbirth. Daniel d. 29 March 1846, age 58. John d. 2 Oct 1836 at age 81, his widow d. 20 May 1838, age 79. One of JohnÕs children was born 6 Sep 1753.

Fanny Sophia Allen was b. 25 Sep 1837, m. Elam Allen, son of Daniel and Chloe Drake French, in East Windsor, and had issue, Daniel Seth French, b. 23 Aug 1854; George Elam French, b. 12 Apr 1858; Susan Sophia French, b. 2 Apr 1864.

Chloe Drake 1793, d. 20 Mar 1855 in East Windsor, CT. Adopted child was Allen Fitts. Others were Nancy A. French, b. 1828 who m. George E. Allen; Daniel French b. 1829; Elam French b. 1832; Allen D. French b. 1835.

DNA Testing

Administered by Julia French Wood. For any questions regarding DNA, please email Julia at juliaFWood@aol.com.

A good source for research would be for a male with the surname French of this line to take the DNA test. It is a simple test that doesnÕt involve blood. A kit is delivered to your house with special brushes for you to take cheek swabs and the tip is injected into the tiny test tubes to be returned to the lab. After the tests that you ordered are completed, in about 4 weeks, you will be notified and can log in to your personal page at the company to view your results and your DNA matches. They may match up with one of the tests shown here: http://www.familytreedna.com/public/french/default.aspx?section=yresults. The FamilyTree DNA website gives a special lower price to those with the surname French. Read about those who approve of it: http://www.familytreedna.com/testimonials.aspx. To get the discounted price for our French DNA Project group, go to http://small-stuff.com/FRENCH/DNA/ and click at the left on "Join the French DNA Project" then place your order. Julia French Wood suggests the 37 marker test (Y-DNA37), but if you want to start with 25, you can upgrade to a higher test at a later date if needed.
