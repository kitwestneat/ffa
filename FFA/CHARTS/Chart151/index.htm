History

This French family starts from North Carolina and Tennessee. There is another unrelated Peter French of Knox Co., TN whose ancestry is on FFA Chart #129. Also see FFA Chart #14 for a French family from Madison Co., KY. Breckinridge Co., KY, was formed in 1799 from Hardin Co. where a Jacob French received 500 acres of land in 1796 from Capt. Jacob Van Meter listed on the Hardin Co. KY Tax List. Many French ancestors of FFA Chart #137 lived in Hardin, Bullitt, and Nelson Counties, KY.

First Generation

1.1 William French, b. ca. 1760. No proof yet. There is a William French in the 1810 census of Madison Co., KY, living with a female (probably his wife), both over the age of 45. He is the only French in the 1810 census in Madison Co. In this same census, Peter French’s name does not appear; however, John French appears in Mason, Mason Co., KY, living with 7 young children under age 10, and no other children. In 1810, Peter would have been 25 years old.

Second Generation

2.1* Peter French, b. 1785 in TN (listed as Tenn in census records) [6] [10], m. Nancy Hanks [1] in Knox Co., TN on 3 Jul 1810. The 1810 and 1920 census records are lost for Knox Co., TN. Peter d. in Breckenridge Co., KY on 31 Mar 1856 [1] [10]. Nancy was b. ca. 1795 in NC, the dau. of Luke Hanks who was b. in VA, and she d. in Breckenridge Co., KY in 1856 [1]. Peter was a blacksmith who was in Madison Co., KY in 1830 and in Breckenridge Co., KY in the 1840 and 1850 censuses [1]. Peter may have been his middle name and not his first name which could have been John [1]. Two sisters may have been in Madison Co., KY with him; this is only a guess [1]. These sisters may have been Elizabeth French, who m. John McDunner on 17 Apr 1799 and Ruthy French who m. Robert Daughtery in 1800, both in Madison Co., KY [1]. Nancy Hank’s grandfather, Luke Hanks, deeded some of his land to a “John French” in Breckenridge Co., KY [13]. Luke Hanks was buried in Union Star, KY, in 1856 [13].

1785-1810    Knox Co., TN
1812-39         Madison Co., KY where his children were born
1818               Nelson Co., KY for a short time when son was born
1840-56         Breckenridge Co., KY, where he died

Nancy Hanks family came from Prince William Co., VA from 1775-1783. They moved to Halifax Co., NC, then Knox Co., TN (where Nancy m. Peter French), and finally to Breckinridge Co., KY [15]. These two families may have migrated together. Peter’s brother could have been William French. This William may have been the son of James and Keziah (Calloway) French of FFA Chart #14, see 4th Generation William French b. 1 Mar 1785. His family also lived in Madison Co., KY. President Abraham Lincoln was b. 12 Feb 1809 in a log cabin on a farm in what was then Hardin Co., KY (now Larue Co.) to Thomas Lincoln and Nancy Hanks; the timeframe is correct for Nancy Hanks, but not the person. However, they did move to Gentryville when Abe was 7.

There is a James French (b. ca. 1801) who was in the Breckinridge Co., KY in 1830 thru 1860 census records who could have been the brother of Peter, but no proof [10].

Third Generation

Children of Peter and Nancy (Hanks) French, 2.1

Sons George Washington, Andrew Jackson, and William Letcher, as well as their father Peter, were all blacksmiths. Andrew Jackson and William Letcher migrated to MO, and George Washington and Peter remained in Breckinridge Co., KY.

3.1 James French was b. ca. 1810-1814 in Madison Co., KY [1]. He signed his will in 1862, filed in 1863 in Breckenridge Co., KY [1].

3.2 Dr. Robert French was b. 1815 in Madison Co., KY, d. 3 May 1884, m. Lucy M. Hawkins on 29 Jun 1847 [1]. She was b. 1822 and d. 1876 [1]. There is a Robert French in the 1850 Madison Co., KY Census.

3.3* George Washington French was b. ca. 1818 in Nelson Co., KY, d. between 1850-1860 in Mystic, Breckenridge Co., KY, m. ca. 1836 to Pherreby in Breckenridge Co., KY [1]. She was b. ca. 1817, d. between 1850-1860 in Mystic, Breckenridge Co., KY. Their seven children were taken care of by relatives or other families after their parents’ deaths [1] [10]. George was a blacksmith.

3.4 Amilda French was b. between 1815-1820 in Madison Co., KY, m1. James Clark on 13 May 1831 in Madison Co., KY [1] [12]. She m2. Caleb Sims in 1845 in Mercer Co., KY [1].

3.5 John French was b. 1820-1825 in Madison Co., KY [1]. There is a John French in the 1850 Madison Co., KY Census.

3.6* Andrew Jackson French (AJ or Jack) was b. 8 Jan 1819 in KY and stayed in Breckenridge Co., KY until 1851 or 1852, m1. Margaret Rawlings on 12 Apr 1843 in KY. She was b. 12 Feb 1824, d. 25 Sep 1844 in Breckenridge Co., KY, one year after her marriage [1] [10]. In the year ca. 1842, Andrew listed with a man named Dr. Jacob S. White in Union Star, KY and worked as a blacksmith. Andrew m2. Nancy Anne (Annie) Hardin on 12 Nov 1845 in Gallatin Co., IL, per court house records [10]. She was 29 Apr 1827 in KY, and d. 15 Apr 1863 in Gentry Co., and is buried at the Gentryville Cemetery in Miller township, Gentry Co., MO [1]. She was the dau. of Joel Hardin and Abigail Rawlings who migrated with them to Gentry Co., MO [1] [10]. She is related to “Indian” Bill Hardin, one of the early founders of Breckenridge Co., KY [1]. Andrew d. 16 Nov 1861 in the Civil War in Gentry Co., MO [1]. Ref [1] has a photo of Andrew’s headstone and that of his wife Nancy, and info on Nancy’s relative, “Indian (Killer)” Bill Hardin, a founder of Breckenridge Co., KY. Andrew was a blacksmith together with his younger brother William in Gentryville, Gentry Co., MO [10]. After the death of Andrew and Nancy, their sons were raised by Nancy’s unmarried sister, Alice Hardin [10].

3.7* William Letcher French was b. 1823 in Madison Co., KY [1] [2], m1. Sophie Oldham Reid, removed to Gentryville, Gentry Co., MO, and then to Moberly, Randolph Co., MO, and had 7 children [2]. William m2. Sarah Hooten Robertson [1]. William was involved in an incident during the Civil War that ended in the death of his brother, A. J. (Jack) French, or a Union soldier, or both, at the French’s blacksmith shop possibly Gentryville, Gentry Co., MO [10]. William got into an argument about the war in 1861 at age 42 with Marion Patton, who pulled a gun. Jack French stepped between them and was shot. Marion Patton got on his horse and rode away [10]. Jack’s son, Lewis, who was then age 9, led his father home where he later died [10]. Ref. [10] acquired a letter from James French (son of William) to his cousin, Lewis, confirming the story. Marion Patton apparently enlisted in the Union Army after the shooting [10]. William joined the Confederate Army, but he died before he returned home, possibly in AR or KY [2].

3.8 Ellen French was b. 1815-1820 in Madison Co., KY, m. _______Cashman [1].

3.9 Lydia French  was b. 1825 [10] in Madison Co., KY, m. Talton Lay (Lea) who was a Union soldier and killed in the Civil War [1]. The census records show that Lydia was actually the dau. of Samuel French and had a sister, Mary Ann (b. 11 Mar 1827, d. 19 Apr 1907 in Richland, Warren Co., IA) who m. David Simpson Bruner and both lived in Breckinridge Co., KY [10].

3.10 male French, may be a son or grandson, born between 1835-1840 on 1840 census [1].
