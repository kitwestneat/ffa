5.3* Peter Eugene French, b. 29 Jun 1938 in Seattle, WA, m. ca. 1969 to Beverly Diane Kochan, d. 5 Feb 2008, they had 3 sons.

Chronology

1938 Jun 29 – Peter was born in Seattle, WA.

1940 census – Peter appears with his parents in Seattle.

1942 Sep 20 – Beverly Diane Kochan was born in Chicago, daughter of Lorraine Wenholz.

ca. 1969 – Peter married Beverly Diane Kochan.

1970 Feb 13 – son Eugene L. was born.

1973 Feb 15 – son Eric Lewis was born.

1974 Dec 27 – son Elliott L. was born.

2007 Jun 1 – Beverly died in Des Plaines, IL.

2008 Feb 5 -- Peter died at age 69 on 5 Feb 2008 in Everett, Snohomish County, WA. He lived at 1205 W. Casino Rd., Apt. A103 in Everett.

Peter "Pete" E. French, published in The Herald (Everett) on 15 Feb 2008 -- on February 5, 2008 the world lost a great man by the name of Peter "Pete" E. French. Born and raised in Seattle, Washington, Pete never went far from his hometown. Sadly, at the age of 69, Pete lost his hard fought battle with cancer.

Pete was a very hard working, stubborn man and all who knew him loved and cared for him very much. He was a private man who loved his family, his work, his friends and his horses. Pete has three sons; Eugene French, of Fort Worth, Texas; Eric French, of Birmingham, Alabama, and Elliott French, of Hoquiam, Washington; and six grandchildren.

Pete worked very hard, for many years, as a diesel mechanic working in the marine maintenance field. Spending many of those years working for Stewarts Marine in Seattle. Pete was a very knowledgeable man who was respected by many people for the accomplishments that he made in his career.

In his off hours Pete was an avid horseback rider. He spent many hours after work, as well as many weekends, taking care of and riding his horse. For years he traveled around the area for many different trail rides. He was well known by the horse rider's circle of people, many of whom cared deeply for him.

I know there is much more that could be said about this great man but there is not enough time to say it all. He will be greatly missed by all those who knew him. Remember the happy times, smile in honor of his memory and know he will never be forgotten.

REST IN PEACE DAD you deserve it.
