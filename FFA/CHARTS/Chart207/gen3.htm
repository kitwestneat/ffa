3.2* Arthur S. French, b. 30 Jun 1849, Sag Harbor, Suffolk Co., LI, NY, d. 1916 (age 66 years) and is buried at the Oakland Cemetery in Sag Harbor.

Marriage

Arthur m. Anna (Annie) L. Buckley in 1877 and later moved to Brooklyn. Anna was b. 1853, d. 1923. Annas parents were born in England and her mother was living with her in the 1900 census, age 75. Her brother, William Buckley, m. Ada Dewey and had daughter Julie Buckley. Arthur and Anna French had son A. Percy, 1879-1908.

Chronology

1870 census – Arthur is age 20 and living with his parents and siblings in Sag Harbor.

1877 – Arthur m. Anna (Annie) L. Buckley.

1880 census – Arthur is age 30 with his wife Annie, age 26, and son Arthur Percy French age 1 living in East Hampton, Suffolk Co., NY.

1900 census – Arthur is 49, living with his mother-in-law Ann Buckley 75, his wife Anna 47, his son Percy 21 (born May 1879), and Mary Hanley 36, a maid, in Brooklyn Ward 23, Kings County, NY.

1910 census – Arthur was age 60 living in Brooklyn Ward 23, Kings County, NY, married to Anna L. French, age 57. They had servant Catherine Sheild born in Ireland.

1916 – Arthur died at age 66.

Buried at the Oakland Cemetery, Sag Harbor, in the same area are: Wade French, Harriet Wade French, Frank C. French, Emma Walton French, Henry French, Hannibal French Jr., Genevieve French, Florence E. French, Cornelia W. French, Hannibal French Sr., Arthur P. French, Arthur S. French, Anna L. French, Willie French, Timothy Rowland French Achenbach, Charlotte Moore French.

3.4 Hannibal French, Jr., b. 5 Jul 1852, Sag Harbor, Suffolk Co., LI, NY, d. 1933 (age 80 years) and is buried at the Oakland Cemetery in Sag Harbor. In 1899 Hannibal and his brother Henry were former directors of the Montauk Steamboat Company.

Marriage

Hannibal married Emily Goodale Myers (maiden name Emily J. Van Etten, 1851-1941) who has a Memorial Cemetery dedicated to her called the Emily F. French Memorial Cemetery, located on Thomas Street, east of Route 114, North Ferry Rd., Shelter Island, Suffolk County, NY. Cemetery notes and/or description:

Memorial Park named after Emily Goodale Myers French who was born in Southampton in 1849, married first Theodore Myers, married second Captain Hannibal French. The Myers family purchased the land for the cemetery. Emily died in 1932 and was apparently moved from the Presbyterian cemetery to the new memorial cemetery. Her family plot is in the middle.

Chronology

1852 Jul 5 – Hannibal was born in Sag Harbor, Suffolk Co., LI, NY.

1870 census -- Living in Sag Harbor with his parents and siblings.

1880 census – Living in Sag Harbor with his parents and siblings.

1897 – Hannibal m. Emily Goodale Myers. Emily was 45 years of age at this second marriage. No French heirs.

1900 census – Living in Brooklyn Ward 30, Kings County, NY, married to Emily in 1897. Hannibal was 47 and Emily was 48. They were living with 4 members of the Myers family who were Emilys children by her first marriage to Myers: Charles Y. Myers 24, Lucille F. Myers 19, Francis A. Myers 15, and Virginia M. Myers 13. Also, 3 boarders were living with them.

1930 census – Hannibal was age 77, the uncle of George M. Travis and Blanche S. (French) Travis who were head of household, living with several borders on South oxford Street in Brooklyn, Kings County, NY. Hannibal was clerk in an insurance company.

1933 – Hannibal died. Buried at the Oakland Cemetery, Sag Harbor, in the same area are: Wade French, Harriet Wade French, Frank C. French, Emma Walton French, Henry French, Hannibal French Jr., Genevieve French, Florence E. French, Cornelia W. French, Hannibal French Sr., Arthur P. French, Arthur S. French, Anna L. French, Willie French, Timothy Rowland French Achenbach, Charlotte Moore French.

3.5* Frank Conrad French, b. 3 Jul 1854, Sag Harbor, Suffolk Co., LI, NY, d. 1927, Sag Harbor, Suffolk Co., LI, NY (age 72 years). Frank was a purser aboard the steamship Shinnecock named after an Indian Tribe descended from the historic Pequot and Narragansett people of southern New England. He and his wife were buried at Oakland Cemetery, Sag Harbor, Suffolk Co., NY.

Marriage

Frank m. Harriet Wade (1856-1928) in 1881, who was also from a whaling family. They had 3 children: Cornelia, Frances, and Wade. She was the daughter of Sarah Wade.

Chronology

1880 census – Frank is living with his parents and siblings in Sag Harbor. He states that his father was born in Orange County, NY and his mother in Moriches, and that he was a merchant.

1900 census – Frank (45) and Harriet (44) were living in East Hampton with their children Cornelia (7), Francis (4), and Wade (2).

1910 census – Frank (55) and Harriet (52) were living with their 3 children in East Hampton, Cornelia (17), Francis (13), and Wade (11).

1927 – Frank died and is buried in Sag Harbor.

1928 – Franks wife Harriet died and is buried in Sag Harbor.

Buried at the Oakland Cemetery, Sag Harbor, in the same area are: Wade French, Harriet Wade French, Frank C. French, Emma Walton French, Henry French, Hannibal French Jr., Genevieve French, Florence E. French, Cornelia W. French, Hannibal French Sr., Arthur P. French, Arthur S. French, Anna L. French, Willie French, Timothy Rowland French Achenbach, Charlotte Moore French.

3.10 Clara Slate French, b. 19 Jan 1858 in NY and living with her father in 1865 in New York after her mother had died. She m. Auguste Mathez, who was educated at Columbia and was a well-known mining engineer who traveled to many places in the world, including Peking on mining expedition. She died in El Paso, Texas and Auguste Mathez died in Denver, Colorado; both are buried in Denver. They had son Forrest Mathez who was educated at Colorado School of Mines and was a mining engineer in Utah. He later moved to Indian Wells, California [11] for many years with his sister, Claire Arkell and her husband James Arkell on a large property. Lived with his parents and siblings in the 1870 census of Sag Harbor, Suffolk Co., NY. Lived with parents and siblings in the 1880 census of NYC. See Ref. [11] in Bibliography at bottom of page. Most of this family and their ancestors and descendants were buried in Denver.

3.12* Eugene Smith French, b. 23 Jan 1862 in Sag Harbor, Suffolk County, NY, died on the field of Battle, at Calcocan, P.I. (Philippine Islands), on 23 Feb 1899, 3 years after his father died. He was age 37.

Marriage

Eugene m. Frankie Reynolds ca. 1895 and had son Hallett R. French. She was born in 1871 in Wisconsin, dau. of Abert and Sarah Reynolds.

Chronology

1862 Jan 23 – Eugene Smith French was born.

1865 -- Living with his father, Stephen B. French, in 1865 in New York after his mother, Mary A. Hand, had died.

1870 census -- Lived with parents and siblings in Sag Harbor, Suffolk County, NY.

1880 census – Lived with parents and siblings in NYC.

1895 Apr 24 – Married Frankie Reynolds at the First Baptist Church in Helena, Montana. She was born in 1871 in Wisconsin, dau. of Albert and Sarah Reynolds.

1896 Mar 14 – Son Hallett R. French was born in Helena, Lewis and Clark County, Montana.

1898 -- The Spanish-American War was short (April to August 1898) and relatively bloodless. In fact, the body count by the conclusion of hostilities was United States 2,664, Spain 5,500. The payoff was deemed worth it; the United States gained the Philippines, Guam, and Puerto Rico; the financial cost was a mere $20 million.

1899 Feb 23 -- Died on the field of Battle, at Caloocan, P.I. (Philippine Islands), on 23 Feb 1899, 3 years after his father died. He was age 37 and a Lieutenant. Caloocan was annexed by the United States during the war.

1899 Feb 23 -- Buried at the Oakland Cemetery, Sag Harbor, in the same area are: Eugene Smith French, Mary Prentis French Cox, William Cox, Stephen B. French Jr., Lester C. French, Stephen B. French Sr., and Julia Prentis French.

1900 census – Listed as Mrs. E. S. French, age 29, born Jan 1871 in Wisconsin, living in Helena Ward 7, Lewis and Clark County, Montana, widowed, states that both her parents were born in New York, had 1 child who she is living with, Hallett French, age 4, and living with her brother Dexter Reynolds and a servant Lena Bnor.

1910 census -- Helena, Lewis and Clark County, Montana, as Frankie French, b. 1871 in Wisconsin, dau. of Albert and Sarah Reynolds. She is listed as widowed. She is living with her parents, Albert Reynolds 62 and Sarah Reynolds 59, and her son Hallett French, 14.

1920 census – Frankie French is living on Blake St. in Helena Ward 7 with her mother and son, and she is widowed.

1928 Apr 12 – Frankie French died in Helena, Montana, and is buried at the Forestvale Cemetery.
