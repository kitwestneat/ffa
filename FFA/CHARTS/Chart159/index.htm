Background and History

The earliest notice of this family was at the Dutch Reformed Church in NYC. This is the same church where Philip French of FFA Chart #131 attended with his family as early as 1693-1727 [1]. It was the North American branch of the Dutch Reformed Church from 1628-1819 [1]. Chart #131 does not go beyond the 17th century, so we are unable to connect these two lines. I did some research on FFA Chart #131 in England in Jun 2010 which I will add to that chart; however, if someone could research that line further on, perhaps of Philippus, Farmar, or Adolphus in the Fifth Generation between 1740 where FFA Chart #131 ends and 1802 where FFA Chart #159 begins, perhaps there will be a connection.

The old Bloomfield cemetery contains a tract of about twelve acres, but only four acres of this cemetery are located within the limits of the town of Bloomfield. We are unsure of the exact location of District 37 in Whiteside County, IL. Former President Ronald W. Reagan was born in Tampico, Whiteside Co., IL on 6 Feb 1911.

First Generation

1.1* William B. French, b. 13 Sep 1802, NYC, d. 7 Jun 1862 in Minnesota City, Winona, MN, m. 25 Aug 1824 Leah (Letty) Marcellus at Madison Avenue Dutch Reformed Church, NYC. Filed for land 26 Sep 1856, Bloomfield, Fillmore Co, MN, d. 1862 in MN [6].

In the 1850 census, William B. French lived in District 37, Whiteside Co., IL with his wife, Lette, age 45 and their 5 children: Benjamin F. 20; Adaline 16; Charles 14; Frances A. 12; and Cornelia S. 7. In 1850, William was a cabinet maker along with his oldest son Benjamin.

1.2 John French.

2.5 William Henry French, b. 16 Dec 1831, d. 14 Oct 1834, age 3.

2.7* Charles Henry French, b. 2 Feb 1835 in MI, m. Sarah Maria Spaulding on 8 Feb 1860, d. 23 Nov 1897 in Arlington, Washington Co., NE [6]. Jun 1840 in MA, and at age 59 in the 1900 census, she is listed as a widow. Sarah was born in Massachusetts and lived in Forestville, Fillmore Co., MIN in the 1860 census with her parents Asoff Spaulding 55 and Martha Spaulding 50, and her siblings George 18, Linton 16, Walter 12, Chas 8.

Children of Charles Henry and Sarah Maria (Spaulding), 2.7

In 1924, the Svendgaard family from Denmark bought 80 from the Clarence French Estates in the March School District on the rural farm. Earlier in 1912 the Svendgaard family purchased 120 adjacent areas to the Clarence French Estate from the Voorhees Family in March School District and built a new house in 1916. The connection to the Voorhees family is quite interesting, in that the French family of NYC, FFA Chart #131, was connected to the Voorhees family also and maybe Clarence’s family will eventually tie into that chart. The Svendgaard family came over in the 1880’s about the same time as the Plugge family arrived.
