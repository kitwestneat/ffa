History and Background

Jacob French (FFA Chart #195), Louisa French (FFA Chart #30), George French (FFA Chart #136), and John French (FFA Chart #129) were most likely 4 children of John French, b. 1705, who was the first of this French family in the U.S.; namely, in Pennsylvania. Marriage

December 20, 1790 Margaret Shively, wed David French. The Jefferson County, Kentucky wedding bond has "David French (X)" The bondsman for the license was Peter Letherman and permission for Margaret to marry was given by "her father Jacob." Descendants have long assumed this was a clerks or David Frenchs error, because her father by family tradition is Christian Shively, and shortly before her death, the October 3, 1866 Owensboro Monitor says her father was "Colonel Shively" of Jefferson County, which is certainly a reference to Christian, not Jacob. Christian lived in Jefferson County for many years, owning land, lending money. Jacob Shively, on the other hand, seemed to repeatedly sell his land there to someone for enough money to get by, and then getting permission from them to continue living on the land.

Margaret was the dau. of Christian Shively [1] and Margaret Washbaugh or Mary Bashore or Brasheres/Brashears [2]. She was buried in Ohio Co., KY in 1866 [2], age 88, on a farm owned in 1934 by Mr. R. J. Hewett. The French Cemetery on Pleasant Ridge, in Ohio Co., KY, has no marker for her [2].

January 1783 -- Petition To Congress For A New State. David French, George French, Richard Clark, John Clark, Basil Clark and hundreds of other inhabitants of the west side of the Laurel Hill Mountains, sent a petition to the President of the Congress asking for a new state to be created from their area and an end to the dispute over the land between Pennsylvania and Virginia. The original document is on file at the National Archives and is listed as a letter from David French and George French inhabitants of the west side of the Laurel Hill Mountains, PA, to the President of Congress, January, 1783. The reference to this from an index at the Archives is: 8 p M247, R62, i48, p. 251. Why the book listed this as from David and George French in the index is unknown, since their names are no more prominent in position or size than any other of the 1,800+ signatories.

January, 1783

Petition To Congress For A New State

David French, George French, Richard Clark, John Clark, Basil Clark and hundreds of other inhabitants of the west side of the Laurel Hill Mountains, sent a petition to the President of the Congress asking for a new state to be created from their area and an end to the dispute over the land between Pennsylvania and Virginia.

The original document is on file at the National Archives and is listed as a letter from David French and George French inhabitants of the west side of the Laurel Hill Mountains, PA, to the President of Congress, January, 1783. The reference to this from an index at the Archives is: 8 p M247, R62, i48, p. 251. Why the book listed this as from David and George French in the index is unknown, since their names are no more prominent in position or size than any other of the 1,800+ signatories.

There was another David French who had bought land in the West Virginia area, and he was the son of Matthew French and demonstrably not the David of Virginia.

David was not listed on Lincoln County, Virginia tax records. In 1786, Madison and Mercer County were formed from Lincoln County. There was a William French in Madison County.

[18] Letter sent by Ref. [8], dated 1934.

The following letter, written in 1934, is one of the items contained in a "FRENCH Family folder" at the Filson Club library in Louisville, KY. It lists the children of this couple and their spouses.

(Transcribed August 5, 2007)

Cary, Miller & Kirk
Attorneys at Law
Owensboro, Ky.

October 23, 1934

Dear Mrs. Bowman:

David French and Margaret Shively were my great-great grandparents. You will find their marriage recorded in Jefferson County under date of December 20, 1790. David French died about 1818. Margaret French lived until about 1870 or later. Mr. John C. Riley of Hartford, who is 85 years of age, remembers her very well. Mr. Robert Bell of Whitesville, Ky. is one of her grandsons. He remembers her very well. A Mr. French living near Buford in Ohio County, Ky. knows where she is buried. He says she is buried in what is known as the French burying ground over on a farm now owned by Mr. R. J. Hewett.

Their marriage record indicates that she was the daughter of Jacob Shively. But I am convinced in my own mind that thru oversight of the clerk in making the record of the marriage license, he either confused the name of her father with the name of her brother or else confused the name of her father with the name of David Frenchs father. There was a Jacob French in Jefferson County at that time according to some records I have observed but which records I do not at this moment recall. I think Margaret had a brother Jacob Shively.

Christian Shivelys will is recorded in Will Book No. I at Louisville. It refers to his daughter Margaret French. It refers to other children and grand-children whom some of the French descendents now living still remember as their Shively relations. I, therefore, think it is certain that Margaret French was the daughter of Christian Shively and that the referred to Jacob Shively as her father was an error of the clerk.

The whole marriage record presents a clerical error, in that it recites David Frenchs name as that of David Fink or Finck. But it is in reality the record of the marriage of David French and Margaret Shively, as wills, deeds and the memory of persons living conclusively show thru their recollections of the older relatives and their children.

The children of David and Margaret French were:
David French, who married Ann Hudson, December 20, 1820
George French, who married Sallie Hudson, December 3, 1827
Margaret French, who married William Hudson, August 20, 1821
Samuel French, who married Fannie Hudson, August 29, 1821
Sarah French, who married James Westerfield, Jun 9, 1824
Martha French, who married Robert Moseley, November 8, 1830
(These were my great-grandparents)

I do no recall the name of the daughter who married Thomas Bell.

These are in addition to Elizabeth Jones. There was one whose marriage record is on file at Hardinsburg. I once found the record and made a reference to it but do not have the reference before me.

In Perrins History of Kentucky, page 1000, there is a sketch of William Henry Shively, who is remembered by Robert Bell of Whitesville, and the Mr. French who lives near Buford. His sketch says he is a grandson of Christian Shively.

Margaret French lived to be about 88 years of age. He remembers that when he was 9 years of age he frequently rode behind her on horseback. At that time she could ride up to a gate, jump down out of the sidesaddle, open the gate, and after leading the horse thru, lead him to a stump and from there jump back into the sidesaddle as spry as a cat.

I have never traced the Revolutionary record of Christian Shively because of the slight uncertainty which has appeared because of the marriage record as to the identity of her father. I have never known the father of David French but at times have been led to surmise that it was Jacob French. Therefore, my membership in the S.A.R. is not thru descent of either the Shively or French ancestor.
